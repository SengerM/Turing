Introduction

Airbnb is a company focused on lodging worldwide. In this report some results from its operations in London, UK, are presented. The raw data can be found in this link. This data contains information on the different listings in London, as well as all the guests reviews from 2010 up to the present.

Objective

I focused the analysis in:

  • Finding which neighborhoods are more profitable.
  • See if there is some tendency for this to change in time.
  • Can the business still expand?

Results

  • Ideal information: Occupancy rate of each listing.
  • It is not available. 😟
  • We do have the number of reviews per month for each listing.
  • Can we exploit the (reasonable) correlation between them? This can be dangerous because:
    • Listings with $\approx 30 \frac{\text{reviews}}{\text{month}}$ obviously have high occupancy rate. ✅ (Relation almost deterministic.)
    • Smaller values have big uncertainties! Is it small because it is not occupied or because it is occupied for long periods?

Furthermore, most listings have $ \lesssim 4 \frac{\text{reviews}}{\text{month}}$:

Because of this I avoided estimating any kind of revenue per listing.

We can sort the neighborhoods by reviews per month and plot them to get a visual idea of the orders of magnitude:

Top neighborhood is Westminster, almost twice as next ones.

How efficient is each neighborhood? Normalize the reviews per month by the number of listings:

  • Westminster among the most efficients, also among highest rates.
  • Hillingdon is the most efficient under this definition.

Evolution in time? Casually Hillingdon seems to be growing at a higher rate than the others:

  • The number of reviews per year in all the neighborhoods seems to keep growing "almost" exponentially each year.
  • The effect of the COVID-19 pandemic is visible: 2020 was at the level of 2016.

Conclusions

  • An overview of the activity of Airbnb in London was presented.
  • The top neighborhoods in terms of number of reviews per month were identified.
  • A simple criterion for efficiency of each neighborhood was defined.
  • Despite all the neighborhoods seem to grow at a similar rate, Hillingdon shows a higher rate which could be somehow exploited (marketing?).
  • The number of reviews per year was growing "almost exponentially" before the pandemic, there is still room to expand the business.
